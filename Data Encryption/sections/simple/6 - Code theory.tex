    Alongside information theory, code theory is at the core of modern CS. 
        To give the reader an idea, the former, due to \emph{C. Shannon},
        deals with how the communication among systems works, 
        providing theoretical tools to solve/mitigate several issues (eg. noise).
        On the other hand, code theory, due to \emph{R. W. Hamming},
        deals with how informations are carried inside the channel.

    In this section we focus on some specific class of codes, 
        describing how these are obtained and what are their propertes.

    Let us note that we assume the reader to have some basic knowledge of 
        linear algebra; precisely, we assume the reader to know what vector spaces are,
        how to sum vectors, and how to work with matrixes.
