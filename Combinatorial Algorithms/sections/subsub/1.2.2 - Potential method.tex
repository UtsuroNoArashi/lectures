    Following the idea of potential energy,
        which in phisics represent the energy stored within the object before this begins to move,
        the potential method make use of a potential function to pay out for the computations.
        More precisely, we define a function \(\phi(I)\), that depends on the specific algorithm, 
        that describes the potential of the specific instance \(I\) of the structure.
        With this formulation, the amortized cost is given by 
        \[
            T_{\alpha}(n) = \sum_{i = 1}^{k} T_{i}(n) + \phi(I_{k}) - \phi(I_{0}),
        \]
        where \(T_{i}\) is the time required by \(i\)-th operation.

    We leave the application of the method to the binary example to the reader.
