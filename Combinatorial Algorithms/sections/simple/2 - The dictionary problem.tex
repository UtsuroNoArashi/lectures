    One of the most common problem in CS, is that of keeping balanced a data 
        structure whose fundamental operations are \lstinline{INSERT, DELETE} and 
        \lstinline{MEMBER}.

    The problem can be analized into two distinc scenario, 
        depending on the existence of a order relation on the elements. 

    More formally, given \(S\) a set of elements of a universe \(U\),
        can we create a data structure for \(S\) such that the operations of 
        insertion, deletion and member are all \(\bigO{\abs{S}}\)?

    In the following a few solution to the problem are discussed -- 
        several more exist but are far beyond our needs.
        As a disclaimer, in what follows we assume the reader to have a basic 
        knowledge \gls{bst} and how the operations of \lstinline{INSERT, DELETE}
        and \lstinline{MEMBER} work on these.
